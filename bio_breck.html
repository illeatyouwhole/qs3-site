Biography

In my academic studies, I focused on the structure and function of the central nervous system with special emphasis on the role of the limbic system in memory and learning, the neurophysiological mechanisms regulating sleep and the organization of the visual system. In my professional career as a toxicologist, beginning with an Industrial Postdoctoral Fellowship at Bioresearch Laboratories in Montreal, I have conducted the full spectrum of toxicological studies including inhalation, reproductive, developmental, chronic toxicity and carcinogenicity studies. I specialized in evaluating the effects of xenobiotics on the endocrine and nervous systems. My involvement with quantitative risk assessment began by leading an industry team in the creation of a software program to conduct cumulative and aggregated risk assessment (CARES) using stochastic probabilistic risk assessment tools. Recently I have integrated physiological based pharmacokinetics (PBPK) models into such analyses. I have also been involved in the development of approaches for integrating epidemiological data into hazard based weight-of-the-evidence risk assessments.
